History

Newcastle has a rich history that dates back to Roman times.

Early History

The city was founded as a Roman fort called Pons Aelius in AD 122. It was strategically located on the River Tyne, which made it an important military and trading post.

Medieval Era

During the medieval period, Newcastle became a center for coal mining and trade, which contributed to its economic growth and prominence in the region.

Industrial Revolution

In the 19th century, Newcastle played a key role in the Industrial Revolution, becoming a hub for shipbuilding, engineering, and manufacturing industries.

Modern Era

Today, Newcastle is a vibrant city known for its cultural heritage, thriving economy, and role as a key player in the Northern Powerhouse initiative.
